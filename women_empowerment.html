Women's Empowerment Through Sport

In India, girls drop out of sports at alarming rates after age 12 due to lack of safe, girls-only training spaces, restricted freedom and family disapproval, harassment and safety concerns, limited role models and visibility for female athletes, and pressure to prioritize marriage and household duties over sport.

The Problem and The Gap

The Barriers

  • Girls leave sport en masse after age 12 due to lack of safe, girls-only spaces.
  • Harassment and restricted freedom limit their ability to train.
  • Intense pressure to prioritize marriage and household duties over personal growth.

The Gap

  • Only 23% of India's total sports participation is female.
  • Girls in sport show drastically better educational outcomes and economic independence.
  • Sport builds resistance against child marriage and trafficking.

What We Do (Our Solution)

Girls-Only Leagues

Creating safe training environments where girls feel free to play, fail, and improve without judgement.

Women Coaches Fellowship

Recruiting and training women to enter coaching roles, ensuring girls have relatable role models.

Changing Mindsets

Engaging families and storytelling to build community support and shift the culture around women in sport.

Confidence Building

Dedicated workshops on self-defence and mental resilience to build agency and self-belief.
