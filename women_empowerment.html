Girls' Empowerment & Safety

70% of Indian girls drop out of public activities post-puberty, creating communities where female potential remains untapped. Sports-based empowerment changes this — building confident, visible, leading women from the ground up.

Support Our Girls
The Problem

The Community Gap

70% of Indian girls drop out of public activities post-puberty, creating communities where female potential remains entirely untapped.

Girls confined to homes after school with no access to public sports or recreational spaces.

Boys dominate all public spaces, leaving girls with nowhere safe to play or gather.

No female role models in villages to inspire or demonstrate what's possible for girls.

Parents routinely prioritize boys for education and activity investment over daughters.

Our Community Approach

Building environments where every girl can play, lead, and thrive.

Girls-Only Sports Leagues

Mixed-ability, all-welcome community leagues where girls play freely without judgment.

Mother-Daughter Workshops

Empowerment sessions that bring mothers and daughters together to build shared confidence.

Women Community Coaches

Local women trained as coaches and leaders — becoming visible role models in their own villages.

Safe Play Zones

Designated, protected spaces in villages where girls can play, practice, and belong.

Girls' Leadership Summits

100 girls per district gathering to develop leadership, voice, and ambition for their futures.

Mass Impact

From individual girls to a generation of confident, visible women leaders.

3,000+ Girls active in sports annually
1,000+ Mothers supporting daughters' participation
50+ Women coaches becoming community leaders
Goal: Girls visible, confident, and leading in communities
