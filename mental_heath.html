Mental Health

India's youth face a silent mental health crisis. Academic pressure to secure good grades, relentless family expectations, social media comparisons, growing unemployment fears, and the complete absence of safe spaces to speak openly leave millions of young people struggling alone.

Donate / Partner
The Problem

The Community Gap

Most mental health conversations stop at elite athletes. India's broader youth population remains vastly underserved.

Government schools lack counselors.

Families stigmatize mental health.

Youth have no safe spaces to discuss fears, failures, or dreams.

Our Community Approach

A whole-community model that reaches students, parents, and teachers together.

School Workshops

Mental health workshops for all students – athletes and non-athletes alike.

Parent Sensitization

Sessions to help parents recognize and respond to youth stress at home.

Community Circles

Safe discussion groups for ages 13–18 to talk openly without judgment.

Teacher Training

Equipping teachers to identify at-risk students early and act.

Life Skills Camps

Camps focused on stress management, emotional intelligence, and resilience.

Mass Impact

Turning individual sessions into community-wide transformation.

5,000+ Youth reached annually through school programs
1,000+ Parents trained to support mental health at home
50+ Schools with mental health ambassadors
Goal: Stronger, more resilient communities
