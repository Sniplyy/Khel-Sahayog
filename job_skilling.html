Youth Skills & Livelihoods

76% of Indian youth are unemployed or in low-skill jobs. Sport teaches discipline and teamwork — but non-athletes need these skills too. Community-based skilling bridges school and work for an entire generation.

Donate / Partner
The Problem

The Community Gap

76% of Indian youth are unemployed or stuck in low-skill jobs — not because they lack ambition, but because no community bridge connects school to meaningful work.

Youth lack digital skills needed for even entry-level modern employment opportunities.

No exposure to the growing sports industry — events, media, coaching, and entrepreneurship.

Parents push only "safe" traditional careers, limiting youth from exploring viable alternatives.

No structured bridge between school graduation and the real world of work in communities.

Our Community Approach

Turning the sports ecosystem into a launchpad for youth livelihoods.

Event Management Training

Hands-on training in organizing sports tournaments, community events, and logistics.

Digital Skills Bootcamps

Excel, social media, and content tools — skills that open doors across every industry.

Sports Entrepreneurship

Incubator program helping youth build micro-businesses in sports and wellness sectors.

Freelance Content Training

Training in sports journalism, photography, and social media content creation for freelance careers.

Vocational Sports Skills

Refereeing, fitness instruction, and sports therapy certifications for direct employment.

Mass Impact

Building a generation of skilled, employed, confident young Indians.

2,000+ Youth gaining employable skills annually
100+ Youth businesses started through incubator
Village economies strengthened by local entrepreneurs
Goal: Parents redefining success beyond "safe" careers
